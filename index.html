context

The island of malta boasts some of the oldest known human structures. Malta's megalithic temples are prehistoric constructions dating back to 4500 BC. However, the construction of these temples spans a long historical period, which implies a great variety in the architecture of these different temples, as can be seen between the two temples shown opposite.

skorba temple

This temple dates from the Skorba phase, which lasted from 4500 to 4100 BC. The temple's architecture is fairly simple, with just 3 apses.

tarxien temple

This temple dates from the Tarxian phase, which lasted from 2900 to 2500 BC. Its architecture is more complex than that of the Skorba phase, with 6 apses.
